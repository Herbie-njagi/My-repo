# Why AI Search Is Changing SEO

## Topic
AI Search / GEO / SEO / Zero-Click Marketing

## Summary

In this episode, Amanda Natividad explains why generative AI is changing search and introduces Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) as an extension of zero-click marketing. As AI-powered search engines increasingly answer users' questions directly, marketers need to think beyond rankings and traffic. Instead, success depends on whether a brand's content can be retrieved, understood, cited, and trusted before users ever visit a website.

Amanda argues that GEO is not a collection of AI hacks but a framework for creating content that AI systems and people can easily access and rely on. She outlines four key mechanics: retrievability (ensuring content is crawlable and accessible), extractability (making information easy to understand and summarize), credibility (supporting claims with expertise, data, and original insights), and public evidence (building authority through mentions and discussions across the web). Rather than chasing algorithm changes, marketers should focus on producing trustworthy, useful content that strengthens both their website and their broader online reputation.

## Key Takeaways

- AI search shifts success beyond rankings by emphasizing whether content can be retrieved, cited, and trusted.
- GEO is built around four principles: retrievability, extractability, credibility, and public evidence.
- Clear, well-structured content with original insights is more likely to be summarized and cited by AI systems.
- Brand authority increasingly depends on off-site signals such as reviews, podcasts, social media, and third-party mentions.
- Effective GEO builds on strong marketing fundamentals rather than relying on short-term AI optimization tactics.
