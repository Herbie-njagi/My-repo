## Summary

This interview explores why AI search is fundamentally changing how businesses approach SEO. While Google still dominates search, AI Overviews and conversational search experiences are reducing clicks and changing how brands earn visibility. Instead of competing solely for keyword rankings, marketers now need to optimize for topical relevance and become trusted sources that AI systems can retrieve, understand, and cite. A major theme is that AI search is probabilistic rather than deterministic. Unlike traditional search rankings, large language models generate responses based on probabilities and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), pulling relevant information from multiple sources to create an answer. Because of this, brands cannot rely on ranking for a single keyword; they must build comprehensive topical authority, structure content for AI retrieval, and ensure their content is technically accessible. The discussion concludes with practical strategies for succeeding in AI search. Rather than chasing short-term AI hacks, businesses should focus on audience research, create comprehensive content around user problems, format content into semantically meaningful sections, and continue following SEO fundamentals like internal linking, crawlability, and authoritative backlinks. ## Key Takeaways

- AI search changes SEO from keyword rankings to visibility across topics and conversational queries.
- Google Search remains dominant, but AI Overviews are reducing clicks and changing how users discover information.
- Large language models produce probabilistic answers, meaning brands must optimize for topical authority rather than fixed rankings.
- AI systems rely on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), combining training data with live web content to generate responses.
- Traditional SEO fundamentals—including crawlability, indexing, internal linking, and high-quality backlinks—remain critical.
- Content should be structured into clear, semantically relevant sections that AI models can easily retrieve and understand.
- Human review is still essential because AI-generated content can contain factual errors or hallucinations.
- Brands should prioritize audience research and real customer insights instead of relying solely on keyword search volume.
- Query fan-out research helps identify related conversational questions that should be covered with supporting content.
- Sustainable AI visibility comes from producing authoritative, helpful content rather than relying on short-term AI manipulation tactics.
