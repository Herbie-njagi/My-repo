## Summary

Lily Ray reflects on her recent conference speaking tour, where she presented on the relationship between traditional Search Engine Optimization (SEO) and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). She explains that many websites are adopting AI search optimization techniques that unintentionally damage their organic search performance. Since AI models often rely on highly ranked search results as sources, weakening SEO performance can also reduce visibility in AI-generated search. She also shares practical guidance on balancing SEO and GEO without sacrificing long-term search performance.

## Key Takeaways

- SEO and GEO should complement each other rather than be treated as separate strategies.
- Some AI search optimization tactics can negatively impact traditional SEO rankings.
- Strong organic search performance increases the likelihood of being cited in AI-generated responses.
- Businesses should focus on sustainable optimization techniques that benefit both SEO and AI search.
- A balanced strategy is more effective than chasing short-term AI search trends.

## Short Quote

> "Do Great GEO Without Destroying Your SEO."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it emphasizes that AI-powered SEO content production should strengthen—not replace—traditional SEO practices. Maintaining high-quality, authoritative content that performs well in search engines also improves the chances of being referenced by AI-powered search platforms, making an integrated SEO and GEO strategy essential.
