# AI Citations vs. AI Recommendations

## Summary

Lily Ray shares findings from her analysis of 100 B2B "best [category]" queries in Google's AI Overviews. She found that while self-promotional "best of" listicles often earn citations, they frequently fail to secure actual recommendations in AI-generated answers. Instead, AI systems tend to recommend established competitors with stronger authority and reputation across the web. Her research suggests that earning a citation and being recommended are two different outcomes, with recommendations carrying greater value.

## Key Takeaways

- AI citations do not always lead to brand recommendations.
- Self-promotional "best of" listicles may unintentionally strengthen competitors' visibility.
- AI Overviews appear to prioritize brands with established authority and trust.
- Google's AI systems distinguish between citing a source and recommending a brand.
- Businesses should focus on building genuine authority rather than relying on self-ranking content.

## Short Quote

> "Being recommended is much more valuable."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it highlights an important distinction in AI-powered SEO content production: appearing as a citation is not the same as being recommended by AI systems. To improve AI visibility, marketers should prioritize building brand authority, credibility, and trust rather than relying on self-promotional content that may ultimately benefit competitors.
