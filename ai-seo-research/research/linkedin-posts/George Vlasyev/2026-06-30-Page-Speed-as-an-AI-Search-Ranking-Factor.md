## Summary

George Vlasyev explains that page speed has become even more important in the era of AI search. Sharing insights from Michael King on *The GEO Blueprint* podcast, he notes that AI systems like ChatGPT may abandon slow-loading pages before they fully respond, causing them to source information from faster competitors instead. He advises businesses to monitor server logs for HTTP 499 errors, which can indicate that AI crawlers terminated the connection due to slow response times, and to optimize page performance to improve AI visibility.

## Key Takeaways

- Fast page loading is essential for AI search visibility.
- AI crawlers may stop waiting for slow pages and choose another source instead.
- HTTP 499 errors in server logs can indicate AI bots abandoned the request.
- Improving page speed can increase the likelihood of being cited by AI search platforms.
- Technical SEO remains a critical component of Generative Engine Optimization (GEO).

## Short Quote

> "Speed isn't a bonus—it's an eligibility gate."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because technical website performance directly impacts AI-powered SEO content production. Even high-quality content may not be cited by AI systems if pages load too slowly, making performance optimization an important part of improving visibility in AI-generated search results.
