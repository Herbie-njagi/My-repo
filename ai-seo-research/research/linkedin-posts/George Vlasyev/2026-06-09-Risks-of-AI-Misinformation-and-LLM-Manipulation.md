## Summary

George Vlasyev shares insights from *The GEO Blueprint* podcast featuring Michael King, discussing the growing risk of misinformation in AI systems. He explains that large language models rely on information available across the web, making them vulnerable to manipulation if false or misleading content becomes widespread. The discussion emphasizes that improving AI technology alone is not enough—users also need a better understanding of how AI systems gather and evaluate information to make informed decisions.

## Key Takeaways

- AI models can be influenced by inaccurate or intentionally misleading online content.
- Large-scale misinformation could affect the quality of AI-generated answers.
- Businesses and content creators should focus on publishing trustworthy, authoritative information.
- AI literacy is becoming increasingly important as more people rely on AI-generated responses.
- Building reliable sources benefits both users and AI search systems.

## Short Quote

> "We don't just need better tech; we need better tech education."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because AI-powered SEO content production depends on creating accurate, credible, and trustworthy content.
