## Summary

George Vlasyev discusses how the concept of "black hat SEO" is changing as AI search platforms become more popular. Unlike Google Search, AI platforms such as ChatGPT and Perplexity do not have formal webmaster guidelines, leaving marketers to experiment with new optimization techniques. Referencing insights from Michael King, he describes AI search optimization as the "Wild West," where practitioners are testing unconventional methods—such as serving pages in Markdown format—to understand what increases AI citations and visibility.

## Key Takeaways

- AI search platforms currently have fewer optimization rules than traditional search engines.
- Marketers are experimenting with new techniques to improve AI citations and visibility.
- There is no established playbook for AI search optimization yet.
- Testing and experimentation are becoming essential parts of GEO (Generative Engine Optimization).
- Businesses should focus on ethical experimentation while monitoring how AI platforms evolve.

## Short Quote

> "We are officially in the Wild West of search optimization."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it highlights the experimental nature of AI-powered SEO content production. As AI search engines continue to evolve without clear optimization guidelines, marketers must rely on careful testing, ethical experimentation, and data-driven strategies to improve AI visibility while avoiding tactics that may become ineffective or discouraged in the future.
