## Summary

Eli Schwartz critiques several SEO agency proposals he recently reviewed, arguing that some agencies are using AI-related buzzwords to justify expensive services without delivering meaningful value. He points to examples such as spam-based Reddit marketing, overpriced AI visibility tracking, and excessive AI-generated content production that did not match the client's business needs. He emphasizes that effective SEO should be tailored to a company's goals rather than relying on generic, high-volume content or unnecessary services.

## Key Takeaways

- AI should be used to improve efficiency, not to justify inflated service fees.
- SEO strategies should be customized to the client's business model and target audience.
- High volumes of AI-generated content are not always appropriate or valuable.
- Businesses should carefully evaluate agency recommendations and understand the actual value being delivered.
- Trust, expertise, and strategic thinking are more important than AI-related marketing claims.

## Short Quote

> "The threat to agencies isn't just AI."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it highlights the importance of quality over quantity in AI-powered SEO content production. It encourages businesses to focus on strategic, audience-focused content and to critically evaluate AI-driven SEO services rather than assuming that more AI-generated content or expensive tools automatically produce better results.
