## Summary

Eli Schwartz explains that having multiple pages targeting the same high-intent keywords can hurt both SEO performance and conversions. He describes how content and product teams often create overlapping pages over time, causing search engines to split rankings and traffic between them. Rather than focusing only on technical SEO, he recommends identifying the page that best serves the business goal and consolidating or removing competing pages to improve both search visibility and conversion rates.

## Key Takeaways

- Multiple pages targeting the same keywords can lead to keyword cannibalization.
- Search engines may divide traffic among similar pages instead of ranking a single authoritative page.
- The highest-ranking page is not always the page that generates the most conversions.
- Regular cannibalization audits help identify unnecessary or competing content.
- Consolidating or removing overlapping pages can improve both SEO performance and business outcomes.

## Short Quote

> "Don't hoard pages."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it emphasizes that effective AI-powered SEO content production is not about creating more content, but about creating the right content. Consolidating overlapping pages helps search engines and AI systems identify the most authoritative resource, improving visibility, user experience, and conversion performance.
