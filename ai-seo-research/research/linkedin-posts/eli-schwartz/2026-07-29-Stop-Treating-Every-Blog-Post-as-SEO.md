## Summary

Eli Schwartz argues that many companies mistakenly label all blog content as SEO, even when it fails to support business objectives. He explains that publishing frequent, keyword-focused articles without a clear audience or user intent often leads to stagnant traffic and poor results. Instead, he emphasizes that effective SEO should focus on making products discoverable when potential customers are ready to take action, while recognizing that content marketing and SEO serve different purposes.

## Key Takeaways

- Publishing large amounts of keyword-targeted blog content does not guarantee SEO success.
- SEO and content marketing have different goals and should be measured using different metrics.
- Effective SEO focuses on helping users find products or services when they have strong purchase intent.
- Content should be created with a specific audience and business objective in mind.
- Businesses should evaluate content based on its ability to generate meaningful outcomes such as signups, leads, or sales.

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it reinforces the idea that AI-powered SEO content production should prioritize user intent and business value over simply generating more articles. AI can accelerate content creation, but successful SEO depends on producing content that aligns with customer needs and supports measurable business outcomes.
