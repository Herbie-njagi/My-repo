## Summary

Eli Schwartz discusses how the growing interest in Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is reshaping the role of SEO within organizations. He argues that traditional SEO should be closely connected to product development because product decisions directly influence search performance, while AEO depends more on building brand authority and external credibility. He also notes that increased executive attention to AI search is creating new opportunities for SEO teams to gain greater organizational influence and larger budgets.

## Key Takeaways

- AEO has increased executive interest in search strategy and AI visibility.
- Traditional SEO is most effective when integrated with product development.
- AEO relies heavily on brand authority, trusted mentions, and public relations.
- Strong brand reputation across the web improves visibility in AI-powered search.
- Growing interest in AI search may lead to larger SEO budgets and stronger organizational support.

## Short Quote

> "AEO is not SEO 2.0."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it explains that AI-powered SEO extends beyond optimizing website content. Success in AI search also depends on building a trusted brand, earning authoritative mentions, and aligning SEO efforts with product development and business strategy.
