## Summary

Eli Schwartz discusses how the growing interest in Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is changing the way organizations view SEO. He argues that while traditional SEO should be closely integrated with product development, AEO relies heavily on brand authority, public relations, and external trust signals. He also notes that increased executive attention on AI search presents an opportunity for SEO teams to gain greater organizational influence and larger budgets.

## Key Takeaways

- The rise of AEO has renewed executive interest in search strategy.
- SEO should be integrated into product development because product decisions directly affect search performance.
- AEO focuses on brand authority, reputation, and trusted mentions across the web.
- Public relations and external credibility play a significant role in AI-generated search visibility.
- Increased attention to AI search may lead to greater investment in SEO initiatives.

## Short Quote

> "AEO is not SEO 2.0."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it highlights the different factors that influence traditional SEO and AI-powered search optimization. It emphasizes that AI-powered SEO content production depends not only on high-quality website content but also on building brand authority, credibility, and external recognition across the web.
