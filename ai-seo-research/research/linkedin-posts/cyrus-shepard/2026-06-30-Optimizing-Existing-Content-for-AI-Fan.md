## Summary

Cyrus Shepard advises against creating a separate webpage for every AI fan-out query. Instead, he recommends improving existing high-performing pages by filling content gaps that better satisfy user intent. He explains that AI search engines are more likely to reference authoritative, well-ranked pages than newly created, low-quality content, making content optimization a more effective long-term strategy.

## Key Takeaways

- Avoid creating individual pages for every AI fan-out query.
- Update existing high-ranking content to address additional user intent.
- AI search engines tend to cite authoritative pages with strong search performance.
- Thin or mass-produced AI content is unlikely to rank well or be referenced by AI systems.
- Create a new page only when the topic has distinct search intent, meaningful search demand, and enough original value to justify standalone content.

## Short Quote

> "Fill the intent gaps."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it provides practical guidance for AI-powered SEO content production. Rather than relying on large volumes of AI-generated pages, it encourages improving authoritative content to better satisfy user intent and increase visibility in both traditional search engines and AI-powered search platforms.
