## Summary

Cyrus Shepard shares findings from a study by Eric Lancheres that examines whether "information gain" influences Google rankings. The research suggests that simply adding new information has only a limited impact on rankings, while pages containing a higher number of unique data points tend to achieve better performance. The accompanying chart demonstrates a clear relationship between original data and higher Information Gain Scores, reinforcing the value of publishing unique, evidence-based content.

## Key Takeaways

- Original information alone is not a strong ranking factor across all search results.
- Pages with more unique data points generally achieve higher Information Gain Scores.
- Content that includes original research, statistics, or first-party data is more likely to stand out.
- Creating distinctive, data-driven content can provide a competitive advantage over generic articles.
- Research-backed insights are increasingly valuable for both traditional SEO and AI-powered search.

## Image Notes

The chart compares pages based on the number of unique data points they contain. As the number of unique data points increases, the average Information Gain Score also increases—from approximately **40** for pages with little original data to over **62** for pages containing fifteen or more unique data points. This suggests that publishing original facts, research, and statistics contributes to stronger content quality signals.

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it highlights the importance of creating original, data-rich content rather than relying on generic AI-generated articles. For AI-powered SEO content production, incorporating first-party research, unique statistics, and original insights can improve content quality, authority, and visibility in both Google Search and AI-generated search results.
