## Summary

Kevin Indig shares findings from an OnPage AI study that analyzed 150 top-three Google search results across 50 keywords and 10 industries. The research found that pages containing original data consistently achieved higher information gain scores than pages relying on generic content. He emphasizes that even a small amount of unique, first-party data can significantly improve content quality and AI visibility.

## Key Takeaways

- Original data is a stronger indicator of information gain than content length.
- Top-ranking pages contain an average of four unique data points.
- Pages with fifteen or more original figures achieve much higher information gain scores.
- Adding original statistics helps content stand out from competing pages.
- AI visibility improves when content includes unique, evidence-based information.

## Short Quote

> "Publish more than 4 real, original figures."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it demonstrates the importance of original research in AI-powered SEO content production. Rather than creating longer articles, marketers should focus on including unique statistics, first-party research, and proprietary insights that AI systems can recognize as valuable and cite in search results.
