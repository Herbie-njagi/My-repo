## Summary

Kevin Indig explains that publishing original research does not automatically guarantee that AI search engines will cite the original source. He notes that aggregator websites can repackage research into well-structured, easy-to-extract pages and receive citations instead. According to his findings, AI models such as ChatGPT are more likely to reference pages that clearly present important entities—especially dates and numerical data—and come from trusted domains.

## Key Takeaways

- Original research is valuable but does not ensure AI citations.
- AI systems often favor pages that present data in a clear, extractable format.
- Dates and numerical values are strong predictors of AI citations.
- Trusted domains have an advantage when AI systems choose sources.
- Building authority and structuring content for easy extraction are essential for AI visibility.
