## Summary

Garrett Sussman discusses the concept of **"Zero Context" content**, inspired by Myriam Jessier's work, and explains why it is becoming increasingly important for AI-powered search. He argues that AI systems often retrieve individual passages instead of entire webpages, meaning each section of content should be understandable on its own. Rather than choosing between writing for humans or search engines, he recommends creating content that is clear, well-structured, and informative for both audiences.

## Key Takeaways

- AI search engines often retrieve individual passages instead of full webpages.
- Each section of content should provide enough context to be understood independently.
- Avoid vague references and unclear wording that depend on surrounding paragraphs.
- Well-structured, specific content improves the likelihood of being cited by AI systems.
- Effective AI content balances machine readability with a valuable human reading experience.
