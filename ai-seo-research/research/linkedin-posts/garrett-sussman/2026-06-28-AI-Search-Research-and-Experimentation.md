## Summary

Garrett Sussman discusses the rapid evolution of AI search and encourages marketers to actively study emerging research while validating findings through their own experiments. He highlights recent developments in areas such as AI visibility, citations, fan-out queries, personalization, structured data, shopping results, and prompt tracking. Rather than relying on a single study or tool, he recommends testing strategies with real business data to understand how AI search behaves across different models, audiences, and search intents.

## Key Takeaways

- AI search is evolving rapidly, creating new opportunities for SEO research and optimization.
- Findings from industry studies should be treated as starting points rather than universal best practices.
- AI search results vary based on the model, user context, personalization, and search intent.
- Businesses should measure their own AI visibility and run experiments to validate optimization strategies.
- Segmenting results by model, audience, prompt, and topic leads to more reliable insights.

## Short Quote

> "Run experiments. Track your own visibility."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it emphasizes an evidence-based approach to AI-powered SEO content production. Instead of following general recommendations, marketers should test AI search strategies using their own data, evaluate performance across different AI platforms, and continuously refine their content based on real-world results.
