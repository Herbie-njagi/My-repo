## Summary

Garrett Sussman argues that AI Search has become too complex to be managed by a single person or solely within an SEO team. He explains that AI visibility is influenced by many factors, including SEO, content, digital PR, social media, reviews, video, partnerships, and product marketing. Citing recent research, he notes that AI search results change frequently across different models, making it essential for organizations to adopt a coordinated, cross-functional strategy that continuously monitors visibility, citations, and brand reputation.

## Key Takeaways

- AI Search requires collaboration across multiple marketing and product teams.
- Visibility varies significantly between AI platforms such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity.
- AI model updates can dramatically change which sources and brands are cited.
- Businesses should monitor citations, brand mentions, competitor visibility, and sentiment over time.
- A structured AI Search program is more effective than occasional prompt testing or isolated SEO efforts.

## Short Quote

> "AI Search strategy needs a coordinated program."

## Why It's Relevant

This post is relevant because it highlights that AI-powered SEO content production extends beyond creating optimized content. Successful AI search visibility depends on integrating SEO, content marketing, digital PR, brand management, and reputation monitoring into a unified strategy that adapts to rapidly changing AI search ecosystems.
