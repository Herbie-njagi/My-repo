## Summary

Wil Reynolds argues that marketers often focus too much on choosing the best AI prompt-tracking tool instead of improving the quality of the prompts they analyze. Based on Seer's user experience research, he shares how consumer interactions with AI are changing rapidly. Users are providing more personal context, writing longer prompts, and increasingly asking AI to complete tasks rather than performing simple keyword searches. These behavioral shifts suggest that AI optimization strategies should focus on understanding real user intent instead of relying solely on traditional SEO-style keyword tracking.

## Key Takeaways

- The quality of tracked prompts is more important than the prompt-tracking platform itself.
- Users are including significantly more personal information in AI prompts.
- Task-oriented prompts are replacing traditional keyword-style searches.
- AI prompts are becoming longer and more conversational.
- Businesses should adapt their AI optimization strategies to reflect evolving user behavior.

> "How can I improve the quality of the prompts I'm choosing to track?"

## Why It's Relevant

This post is highly relevant because AI-powered SEO content production depends on understanding how people interact with AI systems. As users shift from keyword searches to conversational, context-rich prompts, marketers must create content that aligns with natural language, user intent, and real-world AI search behavior rather than relying solely on traditional SEO metrics.
